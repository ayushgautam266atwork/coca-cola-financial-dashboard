Key Findings

📉

Revenue Decline Was Intentional

Revenue fell 34% from its $48B peak in 2012 to $31.9B by 2018. This was a deliberate refranchising strategy — Coca-Cola sold bottling operations back to independent partners, shrinking reported revenue while improving margin quality.

✅

Gross Margin Held at 60%

Despite the revenue decline, gross margin stayed steady at approximately 60% every single year. This confirms that Coca-Cola’s pricing power and brand strength remained completely intact throughout the decade.

⚠️

FY2017 Net Income Shock

Net income collapsed from $6.5B to just $1.3B in 2017 — not due to business failure, but a one-time $5.6B tax charge from the US Tax Cuts & Jobs Act. The underlying business remained fundamentally healthy.

💸

$86B Returned to Shareholders

Over 10 years, Coca-Cola returned $51.8B through dividends and $34.4B through share buybacks. Dividends grew every single year — cementing its status as a Dividend Aristocrat with 60+ years of consecutive increases.

🏦

Rising Debt — Strategically Managed

Long-term debt grew 5× from $5.1B to $25.4B. This was strategic — Coca-Cola borrowed cheaply during low interest rates to fund buybacks and acquisitions, supported by consistent $7B+ annual free cash flow.

💰

Free Cash Flow Averaged $7.4B/Year

Free Cash Flow was remarkably consistent across the entire decade. Even in challenging years, the core beverage business generated strong cash — the defining characteristic of a truly world-class consumer franchise.
